Interactive Timeline

1940 - Lahore Resolution

On 23rd March 1940, the historic Lahore Resolution was passed at Minto Park (now Iqbal Park), Lahore. The resolution clearly demanded a separate homeland for Muslims of the Indian subcontinent where they could live according to their religion, culture, and values. This became the foundation of Pakistan's creation.

1947 - Independence

On 14th August 1947, Pakistan emerged as an independent state after decades of struggle under the leadership of Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah. Millions of Muslims migrated to their new homeland, while the partition brought sacrifices and challenges. Still, it marked the birth of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan.

1948 – Quaid-e-Azam’s Last Speech & Death

In September 1948, Quaid-e-Azam gave his final speeches emphasizing unity, faith, and discipline. Unfortunately, on 11th September 1948, he passed away in Karachi. His death was a great loss to the young nation, but his vision continued to guide Pakistan.

1956 – Pakistan Becomes Islamic Republic

On 23rd March 1956, Pakistan officially became the Islamic Republic of Pakistan after adopting its first constitution. This was a landmark moment as Pakistan declared Islam as the state religion while ensuring equal rights for minorities.

1965 – Pakistan-India War & Defence Day

On 6th September 1965, India launched a surprise attack on Pakistan. The Pakistani Army, Air Force, and Navy defended the country with great courage. This day is remembered as Defence Day, showing the unity and bravery of the nation.

1971 – Separation of East Pakistan

In December 1971, after a civil war and foreign intervention, East Pakistan separated and became Bangladesh. This was a tragic moment in Pakistan’s history but also a lesson in unity and governance.

1998 – Nuclear Tests at Chagai

On 28th May 1998, Pakistan conducted successful nuclear tests in Chagai, Balochistan, becoming the first Muslim nuclear power. This made Pakistan stronger in defense and marked an important milestone in its history.
