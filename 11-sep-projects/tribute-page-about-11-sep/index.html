Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah

“Father of the Nation”

Introduction

Muhammad Ali Jinnah (1876–1948), honored as “Quaid-e-Azam,” was the founder of Pakistan. He was a visionary leader whose determination, integrity, and leadership gave Muslims of the subcontinent a separate homeland. His guidance shaped the destiny of millions and laid the foundation of Pakistan.

Early Life

Born on December 25, 1876 in Karachi, Jinnah belonged to a merchant family. He later moved to London for higher studies, where he trained as a barrister at Lincoln’s Inn. His exposure to law and politics in England shaped his vision and leadership qualities.

Political Struggle

Jinnah began his political career with the Indian National Congress but later joined the All India Muslim League. He dedicated his life to protecting the rights of Muslims, promoting unity, and resisting injustices. His tireless struggle transformed the political landscape of the Indian subcontinent.

Independence

Through Jinnah’s unmatched leadership, the dream of Pakistan became a reality on August 14, 1947. As the first Governor-General, he worked to establish strong institutions and guide the new nation. His principles of Unity, Faith, and Discipline remain a timeless message for every Pakistani.
